// BASE ROM OVERVIEW
🔥
POKÉMON
FIRE RED
GBA · 2004 · KANTO REGION

A GBA remake of the original Red/Blue. Kanto region, 151 original Pokémon (plus Sevii Islands extras), linear progression. The most popular ROM hack base of all time — FireRed's source code is fully open via pokefirered, making it the easiest to mod extensively.

  • Most widely used ROM hack base
  • Open source decompilation (pokefirered)
  • 8 Kanto gyms + Sevii Islands postgame
  • 151 base Pokémon + trades/postgame
  • Linear, beginner-friendly layout
  • CFRU engine enables modern features
  • Team Rocket as main villain
  • No Battle Frontier
🌊
POKÉMON
EMERALD
GBA · 2005 · HOENN REGION

The definitive Hoenn experience — combines Ruby and Sapphire with an enhanced story, both villain teams, and the Battle Frontier. Larger and more complex than FireRed. The Emerald decomp (pokeemerald) is also open source and widely used.

  • Second most popular hack base
  • Open source decompilation (pokeemerald)
  • 8 Hoenn gyms + Battle Frontier postgame
  • 202 regional Pokémon, more variety
  • Dual villain teams (Magma + Aqua)
  • More complex map, Dive routes, Secret Bases
  • Battle Frontier — deepest postgame of GBA era
  • More terrain variety than FireRed
// AT A GLANCE
FireRed is the ROM hacker's blank canvas. Its simplicity and open source code mean developers can add virtually any feature without fighting the base game's complexity. This is why most feature-heavy hacks (Radical Red, Unbound) use it.
Emerald is the richer base game out of the box. The Battle Frontier, dual villain team, Hoenn's diverse terrain, and larger dex give hack creators more to work with narratively and structurally. Harder to mod, but worth it for Hoenn-lovers.
For ROM hacking, FireRed has more finished high-quality hacks due to tooling maturity. But the Emerald scene is catching up fast — Emerald Imperium, Emerald Seaglass, Inclement Emerald, and Emerald Rogue are all recent standouts.

// IS THERE A RADICAL RED FOR EMERALD?
"My goal was pretty simple: create an experience in Emerald that is as close to the experience that Radical Red delivers as possible."
— iriv24, creator of Pokémon Emerald Imperium · PokéCommunity 2024
✓ YES — THE ANSWER IS EMERALD IMPERIUM
Pokémon Emerald Imperium (by iriv24, v1.0 released 2024, updated September 2025) is the direct Emerald equivalent of Radical Red. It ports Radical Red's entire balance framework — type changes, stat updates, ability reworks, learnset changes — into Emerald, then builds new trainer battles on top of Hoenn's structure. It even uses the same damage calculator as Radical Red at dex.radicalred.net.

// WHICH BASE ROM — AND WHICH HACK?
PICK FIRE RED IF...
You want the widest selection of finished, polished ROM hacks. FireRed has the most mature tooling, the most completed hacks, and the deepest competitive difficulty scene. Radical Red is unmatched here.

Also pick FireRed if you prefer Kanto nostalgia or want to learn from the most-documented hack ecosystem.
PICK EMERALD IF...
You love Hoenn and want the richer base game — Battle Frontier, dual villain teams, Dive mechanics, Secret Bases. The Emerald hack scene is growing fast with high-quality options.

Also pick Emerald if you want Emerald Imperium specifically — Radical Red's challenge set in Hoenn's superior base game structure.
WANT RADICAL RED FIRST?
Play Radical Red first — it has the larger community, more documentation, and is more polished at v4.1. Then play Emerald Imperium for the Hoenn experience with the same mechanical depth you already know.

The shared damage calculator means your RR knowledge transfers directly.
